Supply Chain Project Management Tools: monday.com vs ClickUp vs Smartsheet

Supply chain project management tools are not optional anymore. Supplier onboarding, warehouse migrations, ERP implementations, new product launches, logistics network redesigns — these are complex projects with dozens of stakeholders, hard deadlines, and real financial consequences when they slip.

Yet most supply chain teams still manage projects through a hellish combination of email threads, shared drives, and that one critical spreadsheet that Kevin last updated in March.

I’ve managed supply chain projects across FMCG, pharma, and beverage — from warehouse relocations to full system implementations. Three tools consistently rise to the top for operations teams: monday.com, ClickUp, and Smartsheet. This is a direct comparison based on what actually matters for supply chain work.

monday.com for Supply Chain: The Visual Powerhouse

[AFFILIATE: monday.com - https://www.monday.com/]

monday.com wins on one thing above all else: visual clarity. In supply chain, where you’re coordinating between procurement, warehousing, logistics, and sales — everyone needs to see the same picture. monday.com makes that picture obvious.

Why Supply Chain Teams Love It

Board-based workflow management mirrors how supply chain operations actually work. You create boards for:

  • Supplier Management — Track onboarding status, performance scorecards, contract renewals
  • Procurement Pipeline — PO tracking from request to receipt, with automated status updates
  • Warehouse Projects — Layout changes, equipment installations, process improvements
  • Launch Coordination — New product launches with cross-functional dependencies

Each board can have multiple views (table, Kanban, Gantt, calendar, map) and switch between them instantly. Your warehouse manager sees the timeline view while your procurement lead works in the table view — same data, different perspectives.

monday AI for Operations

monday.com’s AI assistant is genuinely useful for supply chain project management:

  • Auto-generate project plans — Describe a project (“Onboard new 3PL partner in Southeast Asia”) and it creates a structured plan with tasks, dependencies, and estimated timelines
  • Smart automations — “When status changes to ‘Delayed’ AND priority is ‘Critical’, notify the VP of Operations and create an escalation item”
  • Natural language formulas — Ask for what you want in plain English (“Show me all tasks owned by the procurement team that are overdue by more than 5 days”)
  • Email drafting — Generate supplier update emails directly from task context

Automation Capabilities

This is where monday.com shines for operations teams. The automation builder uses simple if-then logic with no code:

  • When a PO status changes to “Received” → move to “Quality Check” group → assign to QA team → set due date to +2 days
  • When a task is overdue by 3 days → send notification → escalate to manager → change priority to “Critical”
  • Every Monday at 9am → create a summary of all supplier deliveries expected this week → post to #supply-chain Slack channel

You can build dozens of these automations that keep your supply chain projects moving without constant manual oversight.

Pricing

  • Basic: $9/seat/month — unlimited boards, 200+ templates
  • Standard: $12/seat/month — timeline/Gantt views, automations (250/month)
  • Pro: $19/seat/month — private boards, time tracking, formula columns
  • Enterprise: Custom — advanced security, audit log, multi-level permissions

Limitations for Supply Chain

  • No native resource management — You can’t see if your logistics coordinator is overloaded across 5 projects without the Pro plan
  • Automations cap on lower tiers — 250 automations/month on Standard can run out fast with busy boards
  • Integration depth — Connects to many tools but integrations are sometimes shallow (sync a status, but not complex data)

ClickUp for Supply Chain: The Feature-Dense Powerhouse

[AFFILIATE: ClickUp - https://www.clickup.com/]

ClickUp is the tool that tries to be everything — and mostly succeeds. For supply chain teams that want one platform for project management, documentation, dashboards, and communication, ClickUp has the deepest feature set of the three.

Why Supply Chain Teams Love It

Everything in one place. ClickUp’s hierarchy (Workspace → Space → Folder → List → Task) maps well to supply chain org structures:

  • Workspace = Your company
  • Space = Supply Chain
  • Folders = Procurement, Warehousing, Logistics, Planning
  • Lists = Individual projects within each function
  • Tasks = Specific work items with subtasks, checklists, and dependencies

This means your warehouse relocation project and your supplier rationalization initiative live in the same platform with shared dependencies and resource visibility.

ClickUp Brain for Operations

ClickUp Brain is the most comprehensive AI offering of the three:

  • AI Project Manager — Automatically generates project updates, identifies at-risk tasks, and suggests timeline adjustments based on team velocity
  • AI Knowledge Manager — Ask questions about your projects in natural language (“What’s the status of the Bangkok warehouse expansion?” or “Who’s responsible for the freight audit?”)
  • AI Writer — Generate SOPs, meeting agendas, project briefs, and supplier communications from within tasks
  • Connected Search — AI searches across all your ClickUp content, Google Drive, and connected apps

Automation Capabilities

ClickUp’s automations are the most flexible:

  • Conditional logic — if/then/else chains that handle complex supply chain workflows
  • Webhook triggers — Connect to external systems (your WMS, TMS, or ERP) and trigger ClickUp actions
  • Template-based automations — When a new supplier is added, automatically create an onboarding checklist from template with 30 subtasks, assign owners, and set due dates
  • Email automations — Send emails directly from ClickUp based on task events

Pricing

  • Free: Unlimited members, 100MB storage, limited features
  • Unlimited: $7/seat/month — unlimited storage, integrations, dashboards
  • Business: $12/seat/month — automations, timelines, workload management
  • Enterprise: Custom — advanced permissions, SSO, dedicated support

Limitations for Supply Chain

  • Steep learning curve — The depth is a double-edged sword. New team members take 2-4 weeks to get comfortable
  • Performance with large projects — Boards with 500+ tasks can feel sluggish
  • Feature overload — You’ll use maybe 40% of what ClickUp offers. The rest creates noise and decision fatigue

Smartsheet for Supply Chain: The Spreadsheet Evolution

Smartsheet is the gateway drug for supply chain teams that live in Excel. It looks like a spreadsheet, feels like a spreadsheet, but adds project management capabilities that Excel can’t touch.

Why Supply Chain Teams Love It

Zero learning curve for spreadsheet users. Your supply chain team that has been managing projects in Excel for 15 years can transition to Smartsheet in a day. Same grid interface, same formula logic, but now with:

  • Gantt charts that auto-generate from your rows
  • Dependencies that actually block downstream tasks
  • Automated notifications when cells change
  • Dashboards that aggregate data across multiple sheets
  • Forms for data collection from external partners

Smartsheet for Supply Chain Specifically

Smartsheet has the strongest supply chain template library:

  • Supply Chain Dashboard — Pre-built executive dashboard with KPIs
  • Procurement Tracker — RFQ to PO to receipt tracking
  • Inventory Management — Basic inventory tracking with automated alerts
  • Supplier Scorecard — Performance tracking with weighted scoring
  • Logistics Planning — Shipment tracking with timeline views

These templates aren’t just layouts — they come with built-in formulas, automations, and cross-sheet references.

AI Features

Smartsheet’s AI is the least developed of the three, but still useful:

  • Formula generation — Describe what you want and it writes the formula
  • Content generation — Generate text content within cells
  • Data extraction — Pull structured data from uploaded documents

Automation Capabilities

Smartsheet automations are formula-driven, which supply chain analysts love:

  • Conditional workflows — When a cell value changes and meets criteria, trigger actions
  • Approval workflows — Route procurement requests through approval chains automatically
  • Scheduled reports — Auto-generate and email weekly supply chain status reports
  • Cross-sheet automations — When a row updates in one sheet, create or update rows in another

Pricing

  • Pro: $9/seat/month — sheets, forms, Gantt views, basic automations
  • Business: $19/seat/month — unlimited automations, reports, dashboards
  • Enterprise: Custom — work management, resource management, governance

Limitations for Supply Chain

  • Not a true project management tool — It’s a smart spreadsheet with PM features bolted on. If you need deep project management (critical path, resource leveling, portfolio management), you’ll hit walls
  • Integration ecosystem is smaller — 100+ integrations vs. ClickUp’s 1,000+
  • Mobile experience is average — Functional but not elegant. Editing sheets on mobile is clunky

Head-to-Head: Supply Chain Scenarios

Scenario 1: Warehouse Relocation Project

Winner: monday.com

Warehouse relocations need visual clarity, cross-functional coordination, and clear status tracking. monday.com’s visual boards make it immediately obvious what’s on track, what’s delayed, and who’s responsible. The automation features handle stakeholder notifications without manual effort.

Scenario 2: Supplier Rationalization Program

Winner: Smartsheet

Supplier rationalization is data-heavy: scoring matrices, spend analysis, performance metrics, weighted evaluations. Smartsheet’s grid-based approach handles this naturally. You can build complex scoring formulas, create roll-up dashboards, and share evaluation sheets with stakeholders who are comfortable with spreadsheet interfaces.

Scenario 3: ERP Implementation

Winner: ClickUp

ERP implementations are massive, multi-phase projects with hundreds of tasks, complex dependencies, and documentation requirements. ClickUp’s depth — subtask hierarchies, docs, dashboards, and AI project management — handles this complexity best.

Scenario 4: Daily Operations Management

Winner: monday.com

For ongoing operational workflows (PO tracking, delivery management, issue resolution), monday.com’s clean interface and powerful automations keep things moving without overwhelm. The low learning curve means your entire team actually uses it.

The Recommendation

Choose monday.com if:

  • You value visual clarity and ease of use
  • Your team resists complex software
  • You need strong automations without coding
  • You’re managing 5-15 concurrent supply chain projects

[AFFILIATE: monday.com - https://www.monday.com/]

Choose ClickUp if:

  • You want one tool for everything (PM, docs, dashboards, comms)
  • Your team is tech-savvy and willing to learn
  • You need deep AI capabilities
  • You’re managing complex, multi-phase programs

[AFFILIATE: ClickUp - https://www.clickup.com/]

Choose Smartsheet if:

  • Your team lives in Excel and won’t leave
  • You need heavy data analysis within your PM tool
  • You manage approval-heavy procurement workflows
  • Your stakeholders want spreadsheet-style interfaces

My Pick

For most supply chain teams in 2026, monday.com is the answer. Not because it’s the most powerful — ClickUp wins that fight. But because the best tool is the one your team actually uses. monday.com’s combination of visual clarity, powerful automations, and low learning curve means your warehouse manager, procurement lead, and logistics coordinator all stay on the same platform without constant training.

ClickUp is the better tool for large, complex programs. Smartsheet is the right choice for data-obsessed teams. But monday.com hits the sweet spot for most operations teams.
